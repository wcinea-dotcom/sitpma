Ecological Emergency

Protecting the Flora of the Great South

Haitian traditional medicine relies on nature. If we destroy habitats through deforestation or overharvesting, these ancestral remedies will disappear forever.

Sustainable Harvesting Guide

How to collect plants without killing them.

Leaves and Stems

Never take more than 20% of a plant's leaves. Always leave the terminal buds intact so the plant can continue to photosynthesize and regrow.

Roots and Bulbs

Do not uproot the entire plant! Dig carefully on one side, harvest the secondary roots, and refill the hole to save the main taproot and stem.

Bark

Never strip a complete ring of bark around the trunk (this kills the tree). Cut narrow vertical strips on the north or south side only.

Biodiversity Sanctuary

Macaya National Park

Located in the Massif de la Hotte, Macaya National Park is the last refuge for many of Haiti's endemic plants. It is one of the most biologically diverse hotspots in the Caribbean. Protecting these areas is vital for the survival of our pharmacopoeia.
